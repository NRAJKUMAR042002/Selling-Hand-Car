How is the Mahindra Scorpio N car?

Pros

  • Ride quality remains relatively flat overall.
  • Impressive quick and smooth automatic gearbox.
  • Tall upright seating, space-saving slim layered dashboard.
  • Exceptional pulling power/effortless overall performance.
  • Unbeatable road presence, ruggedness, and rich heritage.
  • Cramped third-row seating (good for children).
  • Lacks some essential features.
  • Top-end models are costly.

Cons

Scorpio N Engine
Specifications

Positives:

  • It is the same engine that powers the XUV700 and is surprisingly effortless to drive with strong pulling power.
  • It is a rear-wheel drive vehicle but is also available in four-wheel drive with either a manual (370Nm) or automatic (400Nm) gearbox.
  • 4XPLOR terrain mode optimises traction for different terrains like snow, sand, mud, or a normal road; gets 4-Low and 4-High gear options too.
  • It has a brake-locking differential ensuring the Scorpio N progresses even in extremely slippery conditions.
  • The six-speed manual and six-speed automatic transmissions get the shift-by-cable technology.
  • The six-speed automatic gearbox is quick and responsive, especially when you are driving in a hurry.

Negatives:

  • Some diesel engine noise can be heard from this 2.2-litre mHawk unit once it crosses 3,000rpm

Scorpio N Convenience and Technology

Positives:

  • The Scorpio N is available with a sunroof that lights up the cabin.
  • The high seating enhances visibility, the front seats are large and supportive, and the driver's seat is six-way electrically adjustable.
  • It gets a seven-inch instrument cluster and an eight-inch touchscreen with a 12-speaker Sony music player and wireless Android Auto and Apple CarPlay.
  • The first two rows feel spacious and comfortable, and folding the second and third rows creates about 786 litres of boot space.
  • The captain-seat version (six-seater) has an armrest and recline function, which is perfect for those who need to be chauffeured.
  • To get to the third row, one can walk between the second-row seats (six-seater) or use the one-touch tumble to fold one seat.
  • It is equipped with a remote start engine, temperature control, and AdrenoX Connect app functionality like geo-fencing.

Negatives:

  • The buttons and knobs lack feel as they do not offer much feedback to the user when operated.
  • The second-row seats do not slide back and forth to make more room for the third-row passengers.
  • The third-row front-facing seats have little knee room, the backrest is upright, and hence, best for kids only.
  • The Scorpio N misses important features such as ventilated seats, auto-dimming IRVM, and a 360-degree camera.

Scorpio Safety

Positives:

  • The Mahindra Scorpio N has received the highest five-star rating from the Global NCAP authorities.
  • It gets front and rear cameras, six airbags, and a driver drowsiness detector that alerts the driver when drowsy.
  • It comes with electronic stability control, hill hold control, descent control, and disc brakes on all four wheels.

Negatives:

  • The Scorpio N misses out on the safety net that a complete suite of ADAS features gives.
