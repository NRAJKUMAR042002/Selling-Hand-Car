What is Old Car Insurance?

Old car insurance refers to a type of car insurance that provides coverage to old cars, second-hand cars, or used cars. Car
insurance for old cars provides coverage against damages caused to the old car due to accidents, calamities (natural/manmade), theft,
etc. It provides the same coverage as a new car insurance plan. Hence, it covers both third-party liabilities and own damage.

Why Is It Important to Insure Second Hand Cars?

It is extremely important to insure an old car. In fact, irrespective of the age of the vehicle, whether it's old or new, buying
at least Third-party insurance is mandatory as per law. Here are the top reasons for buying old car insurance policy.

  • Protects Against Risks:

    A Comprehensive Car Insurance Policy provides coverage against damages to your old car
    in case of mishaps such as an accident, fire damages or damages due to natural/manmade disasters.

  • Mandatory for All Vehicles:

    It doesn’t matter if you own an old or new car. Purchasing at least a Third-Party
    Car Insurance Plan is compulsory to comply with the Motor Vehicles Act. Driving a vehicle without a valid motor insurance policy
    is an offence, and you can be penalised for doing so.

  • Third-Party Liability Coverage:

    The Old Car Insurance Policy covers damages to third-party property or life. If
    your old car causes damage to a third party or injures a third party in an accident, the insurance policy will cover such
    damages/losses.

  • Personal Accident Coverage:

    An Old Car Insurance Plan not only covers your car but also provides coverage
    against injury/accidental death of the owner/driver. For instance, if you are injured in a car accident, then the insurance
    policy will cover the medical expenses incurred due to the treatment of the injury. Note that this is a mandatory cover that
    needs to be purchased separately.

How to Transfer Ownership and Insurance of Second Hand Car

When you buy a used car, the first thing to do is transfer the ownership to your name. Similarly, the second-hand car may come
with an insurance policy that is valid for a few months/years. Hence, transferring the insurance is also necessary. Here are the
steps you can follow to get the vehicle ownership transfer and insurance transfer done.

  • Visit your respective RTO (Regional Transport Office) and submit the application for vehicle ownership transfer.
  • Submit all the relevant documents and pay the fees.
  • Once the RC transfer is done, you can contact the insurer for the insurance transfer.
  • Submit the new proposal form along with the relevant documents.
  • Pay the insurance transfer fees, and the insurance company will transfer the policy to your name.

How to Buy/Renew Used Car Insurance Policy Online

Follow the below steps for buying used car insurance or old car insurance renewal online.

  • Step 1:

    Log on to the official website of the insurance company.

  • Step 2:

    Enter vehicle details such as make, model/variant, registration date, registration number and city you drive.

  • Step 3:

    Next, choose the insurance plan: Third-Party or Comprehensive Insurance Plan.

  • Step 4:

    Enter details of the previous insurance policy, such as policy expiry date, claims raised and No Claim Bonus details.

  • Step 5:

    Next, you will get the insurance plan quote with the premium amount to be paid.

  • Step 6:

    If you have opted for a Comprehensive Insurance Policy, then you can further enhance the coverage by choosing available add-ons at a nominal increase of premium.

  • Step 7:

    Complete the payment process via available digital payment modes.

  • Step 8:

    Once the payment is complete, the Certificate of Insurance will be emailed to you. This way, you can complete the old car insurance renewal process or buy used car insurance.
